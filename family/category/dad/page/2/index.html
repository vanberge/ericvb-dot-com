“Handy” Dad

Tuesday, March 9th, 2010

With BabyVan’s due date inching closer and closer…we found a pretty significant amount of work in BabyVan’s room was waiting on me!

We had the furniture on layaway, pictures and curtains to hang up, and, really, just setting up the room in general. Before we could even start thinking about the setup, the room needed drywall work (a LOT of drywall work), new paint, and new carpet.

Last weekend I finally sucked it up and “got ‘er done”.

I taped the room off, finished up drywall sanding, and prepped my brushes and rollers. After one long evening, we had one coat of paint up and looking very nice. The high quality Behr paint we picked was worth every penny: with the primer mixed in, and my thick painting style – I almost could have gotten away with just one coat… But, we bought enough paint for two coats so there was no point in skimping.

Meanwhile, we scheduled the install of our new carpet. The install went smooth and fast, and the new carpet has made the biggest difference! BabyVan’s room had some cheap carpet with thin padding… And now it has a nice thick soft pad supporting a much higher quality carpeting. The light goldish-brown color works very nicely with the warm yellow walls and wood trim of the room.

With two coats of paint, and fresh new carpet, the room was officially ready to start moving into. We arranged and re-arranged the furniture (and re-arranged a few more times) before we finally got a layout that we both agreed on. Chris did most of the work on the rest of the aesthetics – hanging the curtains, finding fabric for home-made (thanks to Cheryl’s mom!) valances, and laying out all the trimmings and decorations. My parents came to visit over the weekend and they were very excited to see how the room had come along. It made me feel pretty proud to get the “mickeyvb” seal of approval on my basketball sized drywall patch job, and my painting even turned out a lot better than I expected. Only major things left are to get our glider/recliner in and for Chris to hang up her animal frames.

Seeing and feeling this room come together sort of makes it set in…we are really having a baby and it is really going to happen soon! And being able to work on it together was been a very special and memorable experience for me.

One last quick note: we had another checkup this week. Everything’s going well and the heartbeat still came in at 150. After next time we start going every week!

We’re looking forward to it more and more every day. We’ve taken some pictures of the room, and also done a quick video-tour of it using our Flip camcorder. Enjoy!

Ultrasound V2

Wednesday, February 10th, 2010

Finally “Dad” getting around to posting an update!

Today we were excited to have a second ultrasound – the premise of seeing how Baby Van  has developed and grown has been is something I think we’ve both looked forward to since the appointment was scheduled.  Normally I think people may just get one ultrasound at the 20 week mark; but Chris was measuring slightly below her doctor’s expectations.  So, they just wanted to do another ultrasound and measurements to verify due dates, growth rates, and everything else was still in line.

As expected, everything is indeed fine – her measurements are on track for our due date and the baby size rates at the 55th percentile.  The ultrasound was cool!  Baby Van is definitely more developed and has much less free space to move around in.  We still didn’t find out the boy/girl factor (sorry moms) – so that will still be a suprise on Baby Van’s Birthday…  But man it’s just crazy to me the level of detail you can see on the baby’s face/hands/feet with modern ultrasound technology.  Although we weren’t able to get a 3d rendering this time due to lack of  free space and positioning.

After the ultrasound, final checks with the doc revealed normal heartbeats, etc – so it seems everything is on track sailing smoothly.

Chris is now coming up to 30 weeks – so we kick into an appointment every two weeks during this 10 week home stretch…  Man, time sure is flying.  I’ve got a lot of drywall work and painting to do and the clock is ticking a little faster than I’d like to admit.  🙁

The ultrasound technician said that video recording of any ultrasound is prohibited – she seemed like she wouldn’t have been to much of a problem to convince otherwise (since she thought my Flip was a cell phone) but I decided I would follow established procedures.  So, bummer – no video.  Blah blah blah – enough talky.  Here are the ultrasound pics!

Cruise Control

Friday, November 6th, 2009

In more ways than one, things are sort of on “Cruise Control” for the time being…

In the most literal sense, we purchased a new car!

That’s a bit of a big investment and alot of money to throw down with a baby on the way – but we didn’t really have a choice.  I don’t think either of us were up to trying to cram all of our stuff along with a dog and a new baby into either of our two door coupe vehicles.  So, one of them had to get the axe in favor of something more family friendly.

We ended up purchasing a 2007 Chrysler Pacifica (Black, leather interior, very good shape) and are in the process of selling Chris’ Dodge Stratus.  It’s working out perfectly… And so far we are very happy with the car.  It’ll definitely provide a much more comfortable ride all around – and the 3rd row seating gives us the space we’re looking for.

 

Secondly, this time of the pregnancy has felt like we’re just cruising right along.

We’ve had one follow up appointment where we heard the heartbeat again, and Chris has seemed to be getting over the “morning sickness” phase.  The next appointment after Thanksgiving will be the biggie where we get the first ultrasound (20 weeks).  It’s a little weird to me to think that we’re already almost halfway through – but both of us are only looking forward to everything that lies ahead.

We’ll be sure to get some pictures up of our new car – and also look forward to posting our first ultrasound pics after we get them in the coming weeks.

Thanks everyone!

PS – For all those wondering – I think we’re still going to “not find out” whether we’re having a boy or girl – so we’re shooting for the surprise.  🙂

The Reveal

Sunday, September 6th, 2009

If you’ve found this page – you obviously know the news…

If not, then you’re going to know now: We are expecting a baby!!!

Positive!

Just a little background about the situation.  We’d been trying for awhile now, and finally we found out we were positive on my Birthday!  (August 13th)   Ever since, we’ve been waiting and planning a way to tell our parents.  We both wanted to make sure they were the first to know.  So, a labor-day cookout was a perfect chance to tell both our families at the same time.

We had everyone over to our home and had a good dinner together.  Then, I fired up our laptop hooked to our television to show our parents “pictures from our trip to Indianapolis” – But instead they saw the picture you can see above.  It was pretty fun for us to see everyone’s reactions as it took a couple seconds for the news to register…

Everyone was excited.  there were hugs, laughs, tears, and a few beers cracked.  🙂

We spent the rest of the evening together as a family sharing stories about how our parents dealt with the same news; what types of things may be in store for us, and generally just really enjoying a very significant moment in our lives and appreciating the family we have.

Finally breaking the news makes everything seem more official – and we’re looking forward to everything that’s coming our way.
