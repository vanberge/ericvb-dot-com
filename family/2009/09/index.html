The Hangover (minus the fun)

Tuesday, September 15th, 2009

At first glance, you’re probably thinking “What?!? You aren’t supposed to be drinking”

Rest assured, I haven’t been.  But the past couple weeks have left me feeling like I have.  Whoever called it morning sickness obviously did not experience the all day sickness I have.  Luckily, I haven’t actually gotten sick, but have definitely had the all day queasiness. It’s not ever been bad, but just enough to remind you that food isn’t always your friend. I’ve found  stuff that continues to taste good, so lately I’ve been filling up on pretzels, watermelon, grapefruit, and McDonald’s french fries.   According to the lady at the doctor, morning sickness is similar to a hangover; both are based off low blood sugar.  So basically while you’re pregnant you get the same effects of alcohol, just without the fun night before 🙂

Of course, I’ll take the not feeling well, knowing what’s on the other end.  It still hasn’t really set in, but as we slowly tell more people it becomes a little more real each day. It’s crazy to me that we’ve already known for nearly five weeks – I feel like we should have the nursery all ready by now. I know both Eric and I are looking forward to the next appointment on October 6th when we can hopefully hear the heartbeat…at that point I think the reality of the whole situation will be fully realized.  Plus, it will give us the assurance that there really is a little being growing, and not just me growing from donuts!

Overall this has been a good experience – more than anything, I love being able to tell people and seeing how excited they are for us.  Nothing beats telling your grandmas that they get to be great grandmas.  And as more and more people know, that means the fewer “fake drinks” I have to have.

The Reveal

Sunday, September 6th, 2009

If you’ve found this page – you obviously know the news…

If not, then you’re going to know now: We are expecting a baby!!!

Positive!

Just a little background about the situation.  We’d been trying for awhile now, and finally we found out we were positive on my Birthday!  (August 13th)   Ever since, we’ve been waiting and planning a way to tell our parents.  We both wanted to make sure they were the first to know.  So, a labor-day cookout was a perfect chance to tell both our families at the same time.

We had everyone over to our home and had a good dinner together.  Then, I fired up our laptop hooked to our television to show our parents “pictures from our trip to Indianapolis” – But instead they saw the picture you can see above.  It was pretty fun for us to see everyone’s reactions as it took a couple seconds for the news to register…

Everyone was excited.  there were hugs, laughs, tears, and a few beers cracked.  🙂

We spent the rest of the evening together as a family sharing stories about how our parents dealt with the same news; what types of things may be in store for us, and generally just really enjoying a very significant moment in our lives and appreciating the family we have.

Finally breaking the news makes everything seem more official – and we’re looking forward to everything that’s coming our way.
