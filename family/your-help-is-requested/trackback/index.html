Your Help is Requested

This is a call out to any existing parents (or those in the know with baby stuff)…

We are starting our registries, and just basic planning, so I would love to hear from you the baby-related items that you couldn’t have lived without. There is just so much out there that we’re overwhelmed with where to start. I don’t want to end up with 100 burp rags and forget that we need some other essential item.  And because Babies ‘R Us sells the Wii, we need some help staying on track with baby things and not fun parent toys!

So, feel free to post your thoughts, lists, etc.  Any help is appreciated.

    you don’t need a diaper genie. buy diapers in bulk but not at first, they go through sizes quick. get a plastic trash can with a closing lid. drop da diaper in the can, close the lid. easy. add an air freshner for fun.

    you also don’t need a “wipe warmer”. the baby won’t even flinch when a slightly-less-than-room-temperature “wipe” is used.

    get a stroller that comes with a carseat that can be detached and put in the car. usually a base is involved that latches to the car LATCH system or seat belt. mega handy to unlatch carseat from base and transfer to stroller.

  4. Natasha Says:
    November 18th, 2009 at 10:34 am

    Hey guys! I have helped some other friends register before, so hopefully I can be of some help to you.

    1. I really like the Koala Kids jersey crib sheets from BRUs – they are super soft and have great elastic. They come on quite a few different solid colors that will match any decor.

    2. I would disagree on the non-immediate need for a pack n play if you have a dog – it can be very handy to pop the babe into while you’re doing dishes or going to the bathroom. Eventually an exersaucer is excellent for that too and can be easily moved around the house. We also use our pack n play a ton to go camping, over to friends’ houses for the evening, etc. I would recommend sticking with something mid-line (nothing too basic and nothing too crazy fancy).

    3. I agree on skipping the Diaper Genie – the refills are expensive. Safety First makes a diaper pail with a lid for about $15-20 and you can use any trash bags in it. I plan on getting one for the twins’ arrival and adding an air freshener.

    4. We have never used bumpers on Sydney’s crib but I’m thinking about getting some of those breathable ones for the twins’ beds. It has always been a pain when her pacifier falls out between the slats of the crib in the middle of the night!

    5. Not sure who’s throwing your baby shower(s), but our hostess had people bring books instead of cards which was totally awesome! People wrote inside them like cards too. Another idea is diapers instead of cards.

    6. We have the Summer Infant video monitors which are totally awesome, especially for tech-y people like Eric! They are pricey but worth the money – almost all of our friends have them too.

    7. We are big advocates of sleep sacks and are still using them (Sydney is 17 mos.). The Halo sleeveless ones are great and non-flammable. They are often on sale at BRUs. Now we use cotton quilted ones that are great too.

    8. The JJ Cole Bundle Me’s are wonderful for cold weather. Even in the spring/fall you can unzip the top half and just use the bottom half to make the infant carseat cozier.

    9. A bouncy seat is also a must! I prefer the ones that sit a little more upright.

    10. We love having a mirror in the backseat in order to see the baby while we’re driving. Very handy.

    Okay, hopefully I didn’t overdo it. If I think of anything else, I will let you know. Enjoy your pregnancy! Mine is going super.

    11. One more thing: If you plan on nursing, the Lansinoh lanolin cream is a lifesaver for the nips.
